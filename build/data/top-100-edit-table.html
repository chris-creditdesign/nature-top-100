Sheldrick, Gm TK Acta crystallographica section a 37188 2008 None Crystallography This is really a review paper (open access). It's mindboggling how it got so popular in 6 years. WTF? Probably because the author wrote: ""This paper could serve as a general literature citation when one or more of the open-source SHELX programs (and the Bruker AXS version SHELXTL) are employed in the course of a crystal-structure determination."" So basically, any crystallographer - anyone working out the structure of small molecules - all started citing this paper for the software they were using. This caused huge amusement in 2010 when the journal briefly shot to 2nd in Thomson Reuters' IF 'science' rankings, ahead of NEJM, Nature, Cell, etc. on the strength of this one paper.
